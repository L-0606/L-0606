Postoperative chemotherapy helps kill any remaining cancer cells and prevents the recurrence of the disease. Additionally, chemotherapy can be used as a form of palliative care to manage symptoms and improve quality of life for people with advanced osteosarcoma.

The prognosis for recurrent/metastatic osteosarcoma is generally poor. The five-year survival rate for patients with recurrent/metastatic osteosarcoma is only around 30%. This is because the disease is highly aggressive and tends to spread quickly to other parts of the body. Despite advances in treatment options, the overall outlook for patients with recurrent/metastatic osteosarcoma remains poor.
Ongoing clinical trials are being conducted to evaluate new treatments, including immunotherapy and targeted therapy approaches, for recurrent/metastatic osteosarcoma. These trials aim to improve treatment outcomes and extend survival for patients with this type of cancer. Novel agents, such as CD47-targeted therapies, are also being investigated as potential treatments.

The prognostic differences between different subtypes of osteosarcoma are as follows:- Poor Prognosis Subtype (POS): This subtype has a poor prognosis and is associated with higher risk of recurrence.\n\n- Good Prognosis Subtype (GPS): This subtype has a good prognosis and is associated with lower risk of recurrence.- Low Risk Subtype (LRS): This subtype has a low risk of recurrence and is associated with the highest level of differentiation.- Aggressive Subtype (AS): This subtype is characterized by an aggressive and fast-growing cancer that typically spreads quickly to other parts of the body.\n\n- Fused Osteosarcoma Subtype (FOS): This subtype is characterized by the presence of fused bones, which can make it more difficult to treat.
Clinical information needed to diagnose osteosarcoma includes bone pain and tenderness, as well as the presence of malignant cells in the bone. Imaging tests like X-rays or MRI scans can also be used.
The impact of clinical staging on the choice of treatment for osteosarcoma is that it influences the decision of which type of treatment is appropriate. Clinical staging helps doctors determine the severity of the disease and the patient's overall health, which in turn affects the choice of treatment.
